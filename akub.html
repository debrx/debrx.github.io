hello

akub is a seasonal flowering thistle that embodies the essence of Palestine. It is also known as cardoon.

akub works to bring celebrations of the rich diverse ingredients and culinary traditions from the region of Palestine. With great influence from chef Fadi grandmother the food brought to the table at Akub not only capture's the essence of his grandmother with the bold flavour combinations but also uses local ingredients available in Palestine. This combination leads to the uniqueness of the menu offered at Akub. Witht the addition of Britain's seasonal produce and age-old Palestinian recipes each dish is imaginative.

akub has 3 varied spaces for private events and parties. Whether is a buisness lunch, intimate dinner, or a drinks reception the staff at Akub are delighted to accommodate to your needs.

Menu

Akub has 2 menus to choose from. An a la carte menu as well as a brunch menu

Akub is one of our feature resturants with currently only one resturant situated in London 27 Uxbridge Street

Fadi Kattan is the Chef and co Founder for akub. The French-Palestinian chef has been in the hospitality buisness for over 25years. Having his father, a business man that designs and installs commercial kitchens, working along side restuarants he has spent his entire life in resturants.

He learnt the art of cooking from his mother and grandmother, who he draws inspiration from. After being reached out to by his fellow cofounder, Rasha Khouri, in 2018 with the plan of opening a Palestinian resturant in London Kattan's though was "Why not?", with London being one of the most receptive city to new flavours and tastes.


While Fadi was planning the kitchen, menu and wine list Rasha Khouri was finding the site and putting together a team of British and Palestinian investors

While working on an other food cencept Khouri became aware of the lack of Palestinian cuisine despite London's resturant scene being very diverse. She wanted a celebration of Palestinian food and cooking and that is what akub became. She enlisted chef and hotelier Fadi Kattan for this task. Having already won international acclaim for translating traditional Palestinian dishes into fine dining experience it was clear he was the man for the job
